The night-owl question — MISMATHE's verdict

Option A: keep 6 AM rhythm  vs  Option B: study late nights, wake late

✅ Option A — early bird (CHOSEN)

  • Every test you'll ever write is 9 AM — RTs, CTs, FLTs, MHT-CET itself. Morning brain = trained match-fit brain.
  • Your best block (8–12, 4 hrs) stays alive.
  • 6 AM wake + exercise is already your habit — never break a working system.
  • Phone temptation at 8 AM ≈ zero. At midnight ≈ maximum.

🌙 Option B — night owl (parked, not deleted)

  • Quiet house, no interruptions — real advantage.
  • Learn-at-night → sleep → morning revision is good memory science…
  • …but it costs the 4-hr morning block and de-syncs you from 9 AM test timing.
  • Highest reels-risk hour of the day.
Verdict: Option A, with B's best idea stolen — your night hour = light coaching recap, and every morning opens with a 10-min recall of last night's material (sleep-consolidation bonus, no downside). Trial clause: if 2 weeks of tracker recaps show night focus consistently beating morning focus, we run a 1-week night-shift experiment and let the data decide. Test weeks excluded — Saturday nights before a test, sleep by 10:30, always.
